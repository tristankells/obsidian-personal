## 🗓️ Month 1: Lock In Your Foundation

**Weeks 1–4 | Target: 55–65 WPM**

### The Problem to Solve

You can touch type — but inconsistently. Speed gains will stall unless every finger reliably hits its correct key every time. This month, slow down to speed up.

### Core Rule for Month 1

> **Accuracy over speed.** If you're making errors, slow down. Muscle memory built wrong is harder to fix than building it right from the start.

### Weekly Schedule

**Monday / Wednesday / Friday — Foundation Drills (25 min)**

- 5 min warm-up: home row keys only on **TypingClub** or **Keybr**
- 15 min: **Keybr** finger-specific practice (focus on your weakest keys — it will identify these automatically)
- 5 min: Note down any letters/combinations that felt slow or awkward

**Tuesday / Thursday — Accuracy Practice (20 min)**

- 5 min warm-up on **MonkeyType** (punctuation off, short mode)
- 15 min: **TypeRacer** — aim for >95% accuracy, not high WPM

**Saturday — Weakness Work (20 min)**

- Compile your "struggle list" from the week (specific letters, bigrams like "qu", "th", "ion")
- Paste them into **MonkeyType → Custom** mode and drill repeatedly
- Run one 5-minute test at the end and log your WPM + accuracy

**Sunday — Rest or Light Play**

- Optional: A few casual TypeRacer races for fun, no pressure

## 🗓️ Month 2: Build Speed

**Weeks 5–8 | Target: 70–80 WPM**

### The Problem to Solve

Foundation is solid. Now you need to push through the plateau by adding deliberate speed bursts and reading ahead while typing.

### Core Rule for Month 2

> **Practice reading 1–2 words ahead** of where your fingers are. This is what separates fast typists from average ones.

### Weekly Schedule

**Monday / Wednesday — Speed Drills (25 min)**

- 5 min: **10FastFingers** — pure speed, don't worry about errors here
- 15 min: **MonkeyType** (quote mode, longest setting) — push 5–10 WPM above your comfort zone
- 5 min: Record your fastest burst and your average

**Tuesday / Thursday — Accuracy & Real-World (25 min)**

- 5 min: **TypeRacer** warm-up (3 short races)
- 15 min: **MonkeyType** (English 1000 word list) — aim for balance of speed and 95%+ accuracy
- 5 min: Practice the "ctrl+backspace" habit — delete whole words, not letters

**Friday — Weakness Drill (20 min)**

- Review your week's missed words
- **MonkeyType → Practice Missed Words** (after any session, this option appears)
- Custom drill: any bigrams/trigrams still slowing you down

**Saturday — Speed Test & Log (15 min)**

- Take a 1-minute and a 5-minute test on MonkeyType
- Log both — the 5-minute test is your real score
- Celebrate any milestone reached! 🎉

**Sunday — Rest**

## 🗓️ Month 3: Push for 90–100+ WPM

**Weeks 9–12 | Target: 90–100+ WPM**

### The Problem to Solve

The final 20–30 WPM is the hardest. Gains slow dramatically. You must exploit every weakness and stay consistent — no slacking, no skipped weeks.

### Core Rule for Month 3

> **Consistency beats intensity.** 25 minutes every day beats 3 hours on the weekend.

### Weekly Schedule

**Monday — Race Day (25 min)**

- 20 min: **TypeRacer** competitive races — try to beat your last average
- 5 min: Note words that tripped you up

**Tuesday / Thursday — Stamina & Accuracy (30 min)**

- 5 min: **10FastFingers** warmup
- 20 min: **MonkeyType** long quote mode — build stamina at high speed
- 5 min: MonkeyType → Practice Missed Words

**Wednesday — Weakness Drill (20 min)**

- Compile all missed words from Mon/Tue
- Custom drill on MonkeyType
- Focus especially on punctuation (comma, apostrophe, colon) if they're slowing you

**Friday — Speed Burst Training (25 min)**

- Set MonkeyType to **10-second bursts** — push your absolute peak WPM
- Then cool down with a full 5-minute test at your target pace
- Log both scores

**Saturday — Full Assessment (20 min)**

- 1-minute test (peak)
- 5-minute test (sustained average — this is your real WPM)
- Log and compare to last month

**Sunday — Rest**

## 💡 Key Rules to Remember
1. **Accuracy first, speed follows.** Never sacrifice accuracy for speed during practice.
2. **Slow down to speed up.** If you're making lots of errors, drop 10 WPM and rebuild clean habits.
3. **Use ctrl+backspace** to delete whole words — it's faster and trains good habits.
4. **Drill your weak keys.** Keybr identifies them automatically — trust the process.
5. **Plateaus are normal.** You may feel stuck for 1–2 weeks — then you'll suddenly jump. Keep going.
6. **5-minute tests = your real WPM.** Short tests flatter you. Use 5-minute tests as your honest benchmark.
7. **Daily > occasional.** 20 minutes every day beats 2 hours once a week.
