1. **Skim**. Quickly read through the headers, pictures and ending summary.
	- *Important: If the material has end of chapter questions, re-write these in their simplest form (MIQ) and keep them for revision. Look at them while reading the material. Keep these notes!*
2. **Focused Reading.** For at 5 mins without taking notes.
3. **Minimal Notes.** Active recall keywords from the section you have just read, Anki ideas.
4. **Consolidate**. After chapter, close the book, reflect, do chunking (mindmap OR spider diagram) and generate questions. After these steps, okay to open the book and re-review things you are not sure of.
	- *Important: If you have MIQ, close the book and attempt to answer from memory*
		- *If you found some information difficult to recall from memory, consider 1. Utilizing a Mnemonic device (memorable visualization) 2. Creating a Anki card for practice.*
5. **Spaced repetition**. Create Anki of the core ideas, which are worth remembering.
	- *Important: When revising, don't read the material again, instead just read through your MIQ questions (active recall + spaced repetition = ace exams).
