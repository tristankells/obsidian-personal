## 1. The Wheel of Life

Draw a circle and divide it into nine segments: Health (physical, mental, spiritual), Work (mission, money, growth), and Relationships (family, friends, romantic). I like to add a tenth segment labelled _Joy_.

For each category, rate your current level of satisfaction from 1 (worst) to 10 (best), then plot it on your circle. This gives you a quick snapshot of the areas of life that feel strong and those that might need attention.

## 2. Fear-Setting Exercise

Ask yourself: _"What would I do if I knew I couldn't fail?"_

Don't overthink it. Just write down the first thing that pops into your head before your brain tries to talk you out of it. Then take a moment to reflect on what fears or doubts are stopping you from actually doing it, and think about how you might reduce or work around those fears.

## 3. Journaling Prompts

I've put together a whole bunch of journaling prompts that I find useful, but you don't need to do them all. Just scroll through the list and pick whichever prompt feels like it's calling out to you.

Personally, I choose depending on how much time I have. Sometimes I'll spend an hour on one deep question, and other times I'll just jot down a few quick thoughts on a smaller one.

1. What would you do if money were no object?
2. If you didn't care about making money, how would you use your talents and skills to serve other people?
3. What would I like people to say at my funeral? And to what extent am I currently living aligned with that future?
4. If I repeat this week's actions for the next ten years, where does it lead and is that where I want to be?
5. What activities in the last month have energised me, and what activities in the last month have drained me? How can I do more of what energises me and less of what has drained me?
6. When it comes to my work or my life, what is the goal and what is the primary bottleneck?
7. Do I work for my business or does my business work for me?
8. If I knew I was going to die two years from now, how would I spend my time?
9. What's the biggest bottleneck to achieving my next goal and why aren't I working on it?
10. How much do my current goals reflect my own desires versus someone else's?
11. What are some areas in which I could invest more money to make life smoother and easier for myself?
12. What could I do to make my life more meaningful?
13. What do I wish I could do more quickly? And what do I wish I could do more slowly?
14. What backpack am I carrying that no longer serves me?

## 4. Action Steps

At the end of your Think Day, write down the key decisions you've made in this format:

**Before today I was** ___

**But as of today I decided that** ___

**Action points:** 1. 2. 3.

**I will review the Think Day action points** ___

This way you're not just journaling abstract ideas, but actually committing to decisions, concrete next steps, and a date to check in with yourself.
