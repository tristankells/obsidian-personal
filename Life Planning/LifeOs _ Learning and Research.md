You have financial, relationship, career and health goals. Productivity is how quickly your progress towards these goals. The progress you make towards your goals, output, is a measure of how much input (time, energy and money). Be more productive is increasing your output. Focused times and adopting good strategies can decrease the time you take, and increase your output, more efficient. Busy is not productive; lots of input, but likely low output.

## System

1. Personal to you

6. Define how you track your progress
	1. How much progress you are making?
	2. Track
		1. Practice test scores
		2. Body weight
		3. How much your lift

### Step 3: Block time + space to take action.
- Identify your peak time, don't waste.
- "Eat your frogs" during the peak time.
- Try to assign a dedicated place / ritual and remove distractions.
### Step 4: Carry out the repeated action, tracking progress.
1. Take action in your time and place
2. Records your actions tracking system.
3. Do this over and over again.... :)
4. Observe progress.
### Step 5: Avoid distractions
- Todo list
	- Clean house, do the washing, get a haircut, emails.
	- Important, have to be done, but need to not interfere with your longer term goals.
	- BATCHING
- Shiny new goals
	- Stick to your quarterly goals, don't be tricked by shiny new goals.
- Other peoples agenda
- Time, energy and effort are FINITE. Do be productive, we want to maximize the time we spend on productive activities towards our goals.

## Principles
1. Less is more
	1. Less goals, more progress on the most important goals.
	2. Less activities
	3. Goals create overheads, to many goals will make these overheads overwhelm
	4. Busy != Productive
2. Proactive vs Reactive
	1. Plan ahead, set time in advanced
	2. Control, don't be controlled.
3. Slow and steady
	1. Small increment process ALWAYS beats short dashes (diffuse, see [[A Mind For Numbers by Barbara Oakley]])
	2. Brick by brick
4. Be patient
	1. Know and accept your limitations
	2. Meaningful things take time, and most people give up
	3. You need to go through the process; Excited -> bored -> distracted -> tired -> burnt out -> goal achieved.
5. Maximize the machine
	1. We are the meat machine.
		1. Carefully manage stress + rest
		2. Sleep
		3. Nutrition
		4. Exercise
6. Long term productivity
	1. You can achieve anything, but not everything.
	2. Focus on your goals, the THING you really want to achieve.
	3. People over estimate what they can achieve in a year...
		1. But underestiamte what they can achieve in 10 years... trust the system, keep tracking and be patient.
	4. Breaks and phases
# Plan
1. Beginning of every quarter, write down your quarterly goals.
2. Beginning of every week, write down your weekly goals AND review your quarterly goals.
3. Beginning of every day, write down your daily goals (todos) AND review your weekly goals.
