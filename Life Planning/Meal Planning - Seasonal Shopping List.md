## **Q1: January – March (Summer Peak)**
- **Veggie Wins:** Tomatoes, Capsicums, Cucumbers, Eggplant, Courgettes, Sweetcorn, and Green Beans.
- **Fruit Wins:** **Stone fruit** (Peaches, Nectarines, Plums, Apricots) and **Berries** (Strawberries, Blueberries). Watermelons and Rockmelons peak in price-drops during Feb/March.
- **❌ AVOID (High Price):** * **Apples & Pears:** You are buying last year’s harvest; they are expensive and often "floury" until the new crop hits in late March.
    - **Kiwifruit:** Supply is very low; prices can hit $8–$10/kg.
    - **Pumpkins:** Prices are at their highest right before the autumn harvest.
## **Q2: April – June (The Harvest)**
- **Veggie Wins:** Pumpkins and Kumara (newly harvested), Broccoli, Cauliflower, and Brussels Sprouts (starting May).
- **Fruit Wins:** **Apples and Pears** are at their absolute cheapest and crunchiest. **Feijoas** (April/May) and **Persimmons** (May/June) are the seasonal stars. **Limes** are cheapest now.
- **❌ AVOID (High Price):** * **Strawberries:** The local season ends; anything left is an expensive "luxury" punnet.
    - **Tomatoes & Cucumbers:** Prices begin to double or triple as we move away from outdoor crops.
## **Q3: July – September (Winter Staples)**
- **Veggie Wins:** Carrots, Parsnips, Cabbage, Silverbeet, Leeks, and Yams.
- **Fruit Wins:** **Citrus is king.** Navel Oranges, Mandarins, Lemons, and Grapefruit are at their lowest prices. **Kiwifruit** (Green and Gold) are in peak supply and very affordable.
- **❌ AVOID (The Budget Killers):** * **Capsicums:** Can hit $5+ per _single_ capsicum.
    - **Stone Fruit:** Entirely imported from the Northern Hemisphere; extremely expensive and often tasteless.
    - **Avocados:** Prices climb significantly as the old season ends.
## **Q4: October – December (The Spring Flush)**
- **Veggie Wins:** Asparagus (Oct/Nov only), New Potatoes, Spinach, Spring Onions, and Radishes.
- **Fruit Wins:** **Avocados** hit their price floor (often under $1 each). **Strawberries** start dropping in November. **Tangelos** and late-season Oranges are still good value.
- **❌ AVOID (High Price):** * **Kiwifruit:** Prices spike again as winter stores run out.
    - **Apples:** Prices climb as we get further from the harvest.
    - **Cherries:** While they appear in December, they remain at "gift-giving" prices until after Christmas.

### **Quick Reference: The "Switch" List**

If you want to save $20–$30 on your weekly shop, make these seasonal substitutions:

| Instead of...               | Use this (to save money)                          |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| **Fresh Tomatoes in July**  | Tinned tomatoes or Roasted Red Peppers (in a jar) |
| **Fresh Berries in Winter** | Frozen Berries (NZ grown)                         |
| **Broccoli in Summer**      | Green Beans or Cabbage                            |
| **Avocados in Autumn**      | Hummus or seasonal spreads                        |
| **Apples in January**       | Nectarines or Peaches                             |
|                             |                                                   |
