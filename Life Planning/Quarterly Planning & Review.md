# Process
## 1. Review the previous quarter plan
- Ask yourself...
	- *Did you achieve what you set out to? If not, why not?*
- Write a couple sentences with your answers.
## 2. Set goals for the quarter
- Ask yourself...
	- *Which goals will you focus on this month?
	- *What is your theme or priority for the month?*
- Review your backlog.
- See [[Think Day Guide]] & [[Outcome And Output Goals]]
## 3. Pick actions you will take towards achieving your goals
- Ask yourself, *"Which goals will you focus on this month?"*

### Tips for Success

- **The Environment Matters:** Don't do this while doing chores. Go to a coffee shop, book a hotel for a night, or at least put the phones in another room and open a bottle of wine.
    
- **Keep it "Safe":** Agree beforehand that this is a "no-blame zone." The goal is to solve problems, not litigate the past.
    
- **The 20% Rule:** Don't over-plan. Leave room for spontaneity; only "hard-schedule" the most important 20% of your goals.

---

For your very first session, the goal isn't just to plan—it’s to **build a positive habit.** If the first one feels like an interrogation or a boring business meeting, you won’t want to do it again.

This agenda is designed to be **90 minutes** long, moving from "warm-up" to "big picture" to "tactical."

## 📅 The "Inaugural Life Review" Agenda

### Part 1: The Warm-Up (15 Mins)

_Goal: Create a positive, connected atmosphere._

- **The Appreciation Round:** Each person shares 3 specific things the other person did in the last few months that made them feel loved, seen, or supported.
    
- **The "Why":** Briefly share one thing you hope these quarterly meetings will do for your relationship (e.g., "I want us to feel like a team," or "I want us to travel more without feeling guilty about money").
    

### Part 2: The "High-Level" Reflection (20 Mins)

_Goal: Align on what "good" looks like._

- **The Highlight Reel:** What were the 3 best moments of the last quarter? (Look at your phone photos if you need a memory jog).
    
- **The Friction Check:** What is one recurring "small thing" that felt harder than it needed to be? (e.g., "The Sunday night grocery rush always stresses me out").
    

### Part 3: The "Big Rocks" Planning (30 Mins)

_Goal: Claim your time and energy for the next 90 days._

- **The Non-Negotiables:** Open your digital calendars. Input birthdays, weddings, or work trips.
    
- **The "One Big Thing":** If you could only accomplish **one** major goal together by the end of the quarter (Financial, Home, or Health), what would it be?
    
- **The Fun Fund:** Decide on one "Getaway" or "Experience" for this quarter. Book the date **now**—even if you don't have the full plan yet.
    

### Part 4: The Logistics & Systems (20 Mins)

_Goal: Clear the mental clutter._

- **Budget Pulse:** Do a 5-minute check on savings/debt. Are you trending in the right direction?
    
- **Household "Bugs":** List 3 small things in the house that are broken or annoying (a leaky faucet, a messy closet). Assign a "lead" to fix each.
    

### Part 5: Closing (5 Mins)

_Goal: End on a high note._

- **The "Help Me" Question:** "What is one specific thing I can do this week to make your life easier?"
    
- **The Next Date:** Put the date for your **next** quarterly review on the calendar.
    

---

### 💡 Pro-Tips for the First Session:

- **Bring "Toys":** Have your calendars, bank apps, and a physical notebook or a shared digital doc (like Notion or Google Docs) ready.
    
- **The 5-Minute Rule:** If you get stuck on a disagreement, set a timer for 5 minutes. If it’s not solved, table it for a separate "deep dive" talk later so it doesn't derail the whole review.
    
- **Celebrate:** Order your favorite takeout or go for a walk afterward. Associate this meeting with a reward!
    

## 🔍 Part 2: Deep Dive Reflection Questions

_Pick 2 or 3 of these to discuss—don't feel pressured to answer them all!_

- **Energy Audit:** "What activity or habit gave us the most energy this past quarter? What drained us the most?"
    
- **The 'If' Question:** "If we had an extra $1,000 and a free weekend next month, how would we spend it to feel most refreshed?"
    
- **Communication Check:** "When did we feel most 'in sync' lately? When did we feel like we were speaking different languages?"
    
- **Individual Growth:** "What is one personal goal you’re working on that I can help you with (even if it’s just giving you space to do it)?"
    
- **Legacy/Values:** "Are we spending our time in a way that reflects what we actually care about, or are we just reacting to life?"

1. **Define the "Big Rock":** Be specific. Instead of "Save money," write "Save $500 for the emergency fund."

## 🚀 Agenda: Life Sprint 0.1 (The Foundation)

**Time:** 120 Minutes | **Materials:** Shared Note/Laptop, Calendar, Bank Apps

### 1. The Retrospective: "The State of the System" (25 Mins)

_Since this is the first time, look back at the last year generally rather than just the last quarter._

- **Historical Wins:** List 5 things you’ve accomplished together in the last 12 months. (Celebrate your "velocity").
    
- **The "Friction" Audit:** Discuss three times in the last month where things felt "clunky" (e.g., "We had nothing for dinner," "The house felt messy," "We didn't have quality time").
    
- **Stop/Start/Continue:** * What is one habit we should **stop** immediately?
    
    - What is one habit we should **start**?
        

### 2. Financial KPI Setup (20 Mins)

_In Agile, you can't manage what you don't measure._

- **The "Net Worth" Snapshot:** Quickly tally your liquid cash, investments, and debts. This is your starting line.
    
- **The "Burn Rate":** Roughly how much goes out the door every month?
    
- **The Big Rock Fund:** Decide on one "Financial Epic" for this quarter (e.g., "Increase emergency fund by $2k" or "Max out the Roth IRA").
    

### 3. Defining the "Backlog" & Top 3 Epics (30 Mins)

_In this section, brainstorm everything you WANT to do, then ruthlessly prioritize._

- **The Brain Dump:** List everything—house projects, trips, fitness goals, health appointments.
    
- **The Selection:** Pick only **3 shared Epics** for the next 90 days. Everything else goes to the "Backlog" for next quarter.
    
- **Defining "Done":** For your 3 Epics, write down exactly what success looks like.
    
    - _Bad Goal:_ "Work on the backyard."
        
    - _Agile Goal:_ "Backyard patio cleared, new furniture assembled, and first BBQ hosted."
        

### 4. System Optimization: The "House Bug" List (20 Mins)

_Agile is about removing "blockers." What small things make life harder?_

- **Identify 3 "Bugs":** (e.g., The Wi-Fi is spotty in the bedroom; the car needs an oil change; the junk drawer is overflowing).
    
- **Assign Leads:** Who is the "Product Owner" for each fix?
    
- **Automation:** Is there one thing we can "set and forget" this month? (e.g., Setting up a recurring grocery delivery for staples).
    

### 5. Capacity Planning & Calendar Sync (20 Mins)

_Check your "Bandwidth" so you don't over-commit._

- **Hard Deadlines:** Input weddings, work deadlines, and travel.
    
- **The "Sprint Buffer":** Look for "High-Stress Weeks" on the calendar. Agree that during those weeks, household expectations (cooking, cleaning) will be lowered.
    
- **Date Night Sprints:** Schedule at least 3 "Core Dates" for the quarter.
    

### 6. Closing: The Scrum Wrap-Up (5 Mins)

- **Confidence Vote:** On a scale of 1–5, how confident do we feel about these goals? (If one of you says 2, make the goal smaller/easier).
    
- **Immediate Action:** What is the **one** thing we are doing in the next 48 hours to start?
    
- **Reward:** Order your favorite food or watch a show—the "Sprint Planning" is officially closed!
    

---

### 💡 Pro-Tips for "Agile" Couples:

1. **The "Lead" isn't the "Doer":** If you are the Lead for "Financials," it doesn't mean you do all the saving. It means you are responsible for bringing the topic up and checking the status.
    
2. **Handle "Merge Conflicts":** If you disagree on a goal, ask: _"Is this a 'Must Have' for this quarter, or can it stay in the Backlog for later?"_ 3. **Keep it Digital:** Use a shared tool (Notion is great for this) so you can both see the "Goal Tracker" on your phones throughout the quarter.
## 🛠️ The "Definition of Done" Framework

For every "Epic" or goal you set in your meeting, ask these three questions:

1. **Physical State:** What does the space or object look like?
    
2. **Administrative State:** Are the bills paid, forms signed, or files organized?
    
3. **Emotional/Social State:** Have we celebrated it or notified the people involved?

## 📋 Examples for Common Life Goals

### 1. Home Improvement / Organization

- **Vague Goal:** "Organize the Home Office."
    
- **Definition of Done:**
    
    - [ ] Desk surface is clear of non-essential items.
        
    - [ ] All loose papers are either shredded, filed, or digitized.
        
    - [ ] All "tech clutter" (cables, old keyboards) is in labeled bins.
        
    - [ ] One "victory" photo is taken and shared with the spouse.
        

### 2. Financial Milestones

- **Vague Goal:** "Fix our Budget."
    
- **Definition of Done:**
    
    - [ ] Monthly "fixed expenses" (rent, utilities) are listed and totaled.
        
    - [ ] A "Discretionary Spending" limit is agreed upon by both partners.
        
    - [ ] Auto-transfers to savings are set up in the banking app.
        
    - [ ] Tracking app (e.g., Monarch, YNAB, or Excel) is synced with all accounts.
        

### 3. Travel & Leisure

- **Vague Goal:** "Plan our Spring Vacation."
    
- **Definition of Done:**
    
    - [ ] Flights and accommodation are booked and confirmed.
        
    - [ ] Time off is approved by both employers.
        
    - [ ] A rough "Top 3 Things to Do" list is created.
        
    - [ ] Pet/House sitting is arranged.
        

### 4. Health & Fitness

- **Vague Goal:** "Get back into a routine."
    
- **Definition of Done:**
    
    - [ ] Gym membership is active OR home equipment is functional.
        
    - [ ] 3 workout "slots" per week are blocked off in the shared calendar.
        
    - [ ] A "Weekly Meal Prep" system is chosen and the first grocery list is made.

## ⚖️ The "Good Enough" vs. "Gold Plated" Check

In an Agile life, you have to decide when a task is **"Good Enough"** so you don't burn out.

|Level|Description|When to use it|
|---|---|---|
|**MVP (Minimum Viable Product)**|The bare minimum to function.|Low-priority "bugs" or busy weeks.|
|**Standard Done**|High quality, sustainable, and organized.|Most household goals and routines.|
|**Gold Plated**|Perfect, aesthetic, and extra features.|High-value areas (e.g., your primary bedroom or a major anniversary).|
## 💡 How to implement this in your meeting:

When you are filling out your **Goal Tracker**, don't just write the title. Open the "Notes" section of that goal and list 3-4 checkboxes.

> **Example Goal:** "New Morning Routine"
> 
> - [ ] Alarm is set for 6:30 AM.
>     
> - [ ] Coffee maker is prepped the night before.
>     
> - [ ] No phones used for the first 30 minutes.
>     
> - **DoD:** We successfully follow this for 5 consecutive workdays.

## 🛠️ The "Life Scrum Master" Checklist

### Phase 1: Pre-Flight (5 Mins Before Start)

- [ ] **Environment Check:** Are phones on "Do Not Disturb"? Is there food/drink available?
    
- [ ] **Tech Check:** Are the shared notes and bank/calendar apps open and ready?
    
- [ ] **Vibe Check:** Ask, _"Are we both in a headspace to do this right now?"_ (If one person is exhausted or stressed, reschedule. A forced Agile meeting is a failed meeting.)
    

### Phase 2: During the Meeting (The Facilitator Rules)

- [ ] **Protect the Timebox:** If you spend more than 15 minutes on a single "bug" or "goal," intervene: _"We’re getting into the weeds here. Let’s move this to a 'parking lot' note to discuss later this week."_
    
- [ ] **Enforce the "Definition of Done":** When a goal is proposed, ask: _"How will we know, objectively, when this is finished?"_
    
- [ ] **Watch for "Scope Creep":** If the goal list grows to 10 items, remind the team: _"Agile is about focus. Which 7 of these are we moving back to the 'Future Backlog' so we can actually finish the top 3?"_
    
- [ ] **Ensure Balanced Input:** If one person is doing 80% of the talking, ask: _"How does that priority sound to you? Is there anything you’d adjust?"_
    

### Phase 3: Conflict Resolution (The "Agile" Way)

- [ ] **Reframe Blame:** If the conversation turns to "You didn't do X," reframe it as a system failure: _"What part of our current system made it hard for X to get done, and how can we fix the system?"_
    
- [ ] **The "Agree to Disagree" Pivot:** If you can't agree on a goal, propose a **2-week experiment** instead of a 90-day commitment.
    

### Phase 4: Closing (The Quality Check)

- [ ] **Lead Verification:** Does every active goal have one (and only one) Lead?
    
- [ ] **First Step Verification:** Does every goal have a "First Step" that can be done in under 15 minutes?
    
- [ ] **Appreciation:** End by saying: _"Thank you for doing this with me. I'm excited to tackle this quarter as a team."_

## 📋 The "Parking Lot" (Essential Tool)

Keep a small section at the bottom of your note called the **Parking Lot**. Use it for:

1. Topics that are too emotional for a "planning" meeting.
    
2. Research-heavy items (e.g., "Which vacuum should we buy?").
    
3. Long-term dreams that aren't for this quarter.
    

> **Scrum Master Tip:** Whenever a conversation goes in circles, say: _"Let’s 'Park' this for Thursday night so we can finish the agenda now."_

### One final piece of advice:

For your first session, **aim for "Done," not "Perfect."** If you only get through half the agenda but you both feel good about the process, that is a massive win for Sprint 1.


Since the Agile approach works best when you aren't starting from a blank page, the best **First Step** is to conduct a **"Silent Brain Dump"** separately before you even sit down together.

This prevents "groupthink" and ensures both of your individual priorities are represented.

### The 10-Minute Pre-Session Task:

**Instructions:** Separately (over the next 24 hours), spend 10 minutes creating a simple list of "Backlog" items. Don't worry about formatting yet. Just jot down:

1. **3 Friction Points:** What are three things that felt "clunky" or annoying in the last 3 months? (e.g., "The way we handle laundry," "Lack of weeknight downtime," "That broken shelf in the pantry.")
    
2. **3 Desired Wins:** If you could wave a magic wand and have 3 things "Done" by the end of next quarter, what would they be? (e.g., "A weekend trip to the coast," "Savings account hits $10k," "Consistent gym routine.")
    
3. **1 "Wildcard":** Something fun or new you want to try together.
    

---

### Why this helps:

- **Efficiency:** When you start your "First Session," you can simply compare lists. If you both wrote down "Fix the budget," that immediately becomes one of your **3 Epics**.
    
- **Equality:** It gives the quieter partner (or the less "plannery" partner) a chance to identify their priorities without being influenced by the other person's energy.
    
- **Reduced Stress:** You won't feel the pressure to "think of everything" while the clock is ticking during the actual meeting.
